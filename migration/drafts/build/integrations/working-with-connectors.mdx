Kissflow integration uses connectors to help you automate workflows by connecting different applications and services. A connector is a bridge that enables data exchange between your Kissflow account and other applications. For example, the Gmail connector creates a secure connection between your Kissflow and Gmail account. 

## Types of connectors

Kissflow offers two types of connectors:

**Internal connectors**: These connectors are built in-house and help you work with data within Kissflow and connect to external applications. Some examples of internal connectors are:

-   [Kissflow Process](/t/h7h4akh/kissflow-process-connector)
-   [Kissflow Board](/t/83h4af4/kissflow-board-connector)
-   [Scheduler by Kissflow](/t/q6h4af8)
-   [Webhooks by Kissflow](/t/60h4af5/webhooks-by-kissflow)
-   [Email connector](/t/q6h4akq/email-connector)

**External connectors**: These connectors integrate Kissflow with popular cloud applications that you use regularly and work with on a day-to-day basis, such as:

-   [Gmail](/t/x2h4af2/integrate-with-gmail)
-   [Google Sheets](/t/m1h4afq/google-sheets-connector)
-   [Google Calendar](/t/h7h4afg/google-calendar-connector)
-   [Slack](/t/x2h4afh/slack-connector)
-   [HubSpot](/t/p8h4afs/hubspot-connector)

![](/migration-assets/working-with-connectors/1.gif)

Connectors also have events associated with them. Based on the use cases they solve, they are categorized as:

-   Triggers
-   Actions
-   Search actions

### Triggers and Actions

Kissflow Integration supports a number of triggers and actions out of the box. The list of triggers and actions is available [here](/t/60h4aln/list-of-triggers-and-actions).

> **Note:**
> 
> The types of triggers and actions available depend on the connector you choose to work with. Some connectors may only support actions, while others support both triggers and actions.
