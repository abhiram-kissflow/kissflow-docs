## What is Google Calendar?

Google Calendar is a popular time-management and scheduling calendar service developed by Google. With its web and mobile apps, you can organize and share your schedule with friends and colleagues.

### About Kissflow's Google Calendar integration

Kissflow Integration's Google Calendar connector lets you connect Google Calendar instantly with other Kissflow apps and popular third-party applications to automate your work. To integrate with Google Calendar, you must have a valid Google Calendar account and be a paid customer of Kissflow. You can use the Google Calendar connector to receive data from other flows or apps to perform specific actions using Google Calendar. Google Calendar connector does not support any trigger events for now.

### Actions in Google Calendar connector

**Action event**

**Description**

Add a quick event

This action creates an event and automatically fills the date, time and description after parsing the available text data.

Create a detailed event

This action creates a detailed event by defining each fields.

Update an event

This action modifies an existing event by updating all filled fields.

Add attendees to an event

This action invites one or more participants to a current event.

Create a calendar

This action creates a new calendar.

Delete an event

This action deletes an event from the calendar.

## Google Calendar connector as an action step

1.  In your Kissflow account, click the **Create** button in the left navigation panel and select **Integration** from the list. Enter a unique name and description for your integration and click **[Create](/t/60h4akx/creating-an-integration)**. 
    
2.  Once inside your editor, set up a trigger step, for example, a Kissflow Scheduler trigger event, **Schedule daily.**
3.  After setting up your trigger step above, click the Add button (![](/migration-assets/google-calendar-connector/1.png) ) next to set up your action step. Search for Google Calendar and select the desired action from the available list. You can find the list of supported actions above.  ![](/migration-assets/google-calendar-connector/2.png) 
4.  After choosing your action event, authenticate the connector. Click **+Add an account** and sign in using your Google account. Kissflow uses Google's OAuth for authentication.
5.  After authenticating your Google Calendar account, add relevant values against the Google Calendar form fields. You can hit Refresh fields to view all the updated fields in the selected Google Calendar form. You can also map Google Calendar fields from your trigger step or fields from your previous action steps. These values will be automatically added to item fields after the action is executed.![](/migration-assets/google-calendar-connector/3.png)
6.  After a successful configuration, test whether your Google Calendar action step is set up correctly by clicking the **T****est** button. The connector will pull field data from your selected Google Calendar account and display it as a JSON output. Similar to the trigger step, errors are notified to you right away. Fix them before retesting the action step.

## Setting up a Google Calendar connector

After creating a custom workflow with your Google Calendar connector's action steps, switch on the toggle button on the top right corner to activate the integration run. Once a trigger event occurs, the Google Calendar action is activated.

To ensure your integrations are properly configured and tested, review our [best practices guide](/t/p8yzsxn/best-practices-for-using-kissflow-integrations) for tips on building robust and maintainable solutions.
