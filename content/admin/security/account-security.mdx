# Account security

<PlanBadge plans={["basic", "enterprise"]} />

[Super Admins and IAM Admins](/docs/admin/users/user-roles-and-permissions) manage sign-in, sign-up, password, and session security for your account from **Account Administration** > **Account security**.

## Manage sign-in preferences

Your account has a unique URL that users sign in from. Go to **Account security** > **Sign in preferences** to see the sign-in options available to your users and to restrict which ones are allowed.

### Sign in with Kissflow

**Email and password** is the conventional method — users enter the email address and password they set during sign-up or after a [password reset](/docs/admin/users/adding-and-managing-users).

#### Configure a password policy

You can enforce a password policy for every user in your account to reduce the risk of weak passwords.

1. Go to **Account security** > **Sign in preferences** > **Password policy** > **Configure**.
2. Set your requirements:
   - **Minimum number of characters** — the shortest password Kissflow will accept.
   - **Password complexity** — require a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
   - **Password history to remember** — stop users from reusing their recent passwords.
   - **Password expiry** — how often users must update their password.
3. Click **Update** to save your requirements.

<Callout type="info">
  Click **Reset to default** at any time to revert to Kissflow's default password requirements.
</Callout>

![Password policy configuration panel with complexity, history, and expiry options](/migration-assets/account-security/6.gif)

### Sign in with Google and Microsoft

Kissflow supports OAuth-based sign-in with **Google** and **Microsoft** accounts. On the **Sign in preferences** page, turn on **Google** or **Microsoft** to let users sign in with that account.

<Callout type="info">
  Contact Kissflow support if you want to configure another OAuth provider for your account.
</Callout>

### Sign in with SAML

Organizations using a SAML-based SSO provider (for example, Okta or OneLogin) can configure it for their Kissflow account.

Click **Configure SAML** next to **Sign in with SAML** and enter the following details:

- **Identity provider (IdP) URL** — the sign-in URL from your SAML provider. Users are redirected here when they click the SSO button on the sign-in page.
- **Sign-out URL** (optional) — where to send users after they sign out of the SAML provider and Kissflow.
- **Security key** — the SHA2 fingerprint of the SAML signed token certificate from your SAML server, used to decrypt data from your SAML provider.
- **Consumer assertion URL** — the URL you give to your SAML provider. It's unique to your account.
- **User field where the SAML identifier is stored** — the column in User Management where Kissflow stores each user's unique SAML identifier.
- **Create users when they exist in the IdP** — automatically add users after they verify their identity with your IdP.

Click **Save** to enable SSO using SAML.

## Inactive session timeout

The inactive session timeout setting signs users out automatically after a period of inactivity, reducing the risk of an unattended session being misused.

Kissflow configures this separately **for web** and **for mobile**:

- **Web** — choose an interval from 1 hour up to 24 hours.
- **Mobile** — choose an interval from 1 day up to 1 month.

The timer only runs while a user is inactive and resets as soon as activity resumes. Once the selected limit is reached, the user is signed out automatically.

## Manage sign-up preferences

Instead of inviting users one by one, you can open your Kissflow sign-up process to anyone with the link.

1. Go to **Account security** > **Sign-up preferences**.
2. Turn on **Allow new signups**.

![Sign-up preferences page with the Allow new signups toggle and shareable URL](/migration-assets/account-security/3.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

Anyone who visits the sign-up URL is prompted for their name and email address. Depending on your account's approval setting — configured on the same **Sign-up preferences** page — new sign-ups may need a Super Admin or IAM Admin to review and activate them from **User Management** before they get an active license.

To stop accepting new sign-ups through this URL, turn off **Allow new signups** and click **Save**.

## Access key setting

Cap how long an access key can stay valid when it's created for a service account in your Kissflow account.

1. Go to **Account Administration** > **Account security** > **Access key setting**.
2. Choose an expiry limit: **6 months**, **1 year**, or **2 years**. The default, **No limit**, leaves expiry uncapped.

![Access key setting dropdown with expiry limit options](/migration-assets/account-security/5.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

<Callout type="warn">
  This limit applies only to access keys created after you change it. Keys that already exist keep their original expiry.
</Callout>
