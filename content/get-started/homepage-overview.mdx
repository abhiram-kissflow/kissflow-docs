# Homepage overview

<PlanBadge plans={["basic", "enterprise"]} />

Your homepage is your starting point after [signing in](/docs/get-started/signing-in) to Kissflow. It gives you an at-a-glance summary of your items, your workflows, and other key information, so you can pick up where you left off without digging through menus.

![Kissflow homepage with the top navigation bar and default widget dashboard](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/1.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

## The top bar

Across the top of every page, you have quick access to:

- **[Global search](/docs/get-started/contextual-global-search)** — search across items, workflows, and apps by name or keyword.
- **Help** — click the help icon to open the help documentation or reach the Kissflow community.
- **[Notifications](/docs/use/personal-settings/viewing-notifications)** — the bell icon shows updates on items assigned to you and other account activity.
- **Your profile** — click your profile picture to get to **My settings**, **Themes**, or to sign out.

## Ask: Kissflow's AI agent

Ask lets you query your workflow data using natural language and get reports and insights back instantly, without building a report by hand. Click **Ask** on your homepage to start a conversation.

Before you can ask a question, add context: click **+ Add context** and select the processes, boards, apps, or datasets you want the AI to draw on. You can select up to **3 workflows** at a time.

<Callout type="info">
  Only workflows you have sufficient access to are available to add as context. If a workflow you expect to see is missing, check your permissions on it.
</Callout>

![Adding context and asking a natural-language question in the Ask panel](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/2.gif)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

Once you've added context, type your question in plain language. Ask interprets your intent, identifies the relevant fields in the selected workflows, queries the data, and returns a response.

The conversation doesn't reset after each question — Ask keeps up to 40 previous messages as context, so you can ask follow-up questions without repeating yourself.

### Generating a report

You can also use Ask to create [reports](/category/reports-in-analytics) with a simple prompt. When Ask generates a report, click **Edit and save** to send it to the Analytics report builder, where you can refine it further. Saved reports show up under **Analytics > Reports**.

![Editing and saving an Ask-generated report into the Analytics report builder](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/11.gif)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

### Checking a response

Every response comes with a plain-language explanation of the result. Click **Show details** to see exactly how Ask got there:

- **Sources** — the workflows used and the fields referenced.
- **Query** — the exact query run against the data.

![Show details panel with Sources and Query tabs for an Ask response](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/3.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

You can rate any response with a thumbs up or thumbs down, and optionally add more detail — whether the response was accurate, how fast it was, and any other comments. This feedback helps improve Ask over time.

![Feedback options on an Ask response, including thumbs up, thumbs down, and additional comments](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/5.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

Use **More options** to open the help documentation or clear the current conversation. You can also expand the Ask interface for a more focused, full-screen view.

![More options menu in Ask with links to help documentation and clear conversation](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/7.gif)

## Default widgets

By default, your homepage shows five widgets, each using fixed system fields as columns:

- **Items in your queue** — items assigned to you.
- **Items created by you** — items you've initiated.
- **Recently accessed** — items you opened most recently.
- **Tagged comments** — comments where you've been mentioned.
- **My to-do** — items you've marked as to-do.

![Default homepage dashboard with the five standard widgets](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/12.gif)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

### Marking items as to-do

Open any process or board item assigned to you and use **Mark as to-do** (or **Remove from to-do**) at the top of the item. You can do this directly from the **Items in your queue** widget. Items you've marked appear in the **My to-do** widget for easier tracking.

![Marking an item as to-do from its header, and the item appearing under My to-do](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/8.gif)

## Customizing your homepage with widgets

<Callout type="info">
  Widget customization is an Enterprise feature. On the Basic plan, your homepage always shows the five default widgets.
</Callout>

If your account has customization enabled, you can add, remove, and rearrange widgets to build a dashboard that matches how you work. Click **Customize** at the top right of the homepage to open the widget picker, drag the widgets you want into your dashboard, arrange them, and click **Save**.

![Customizing the homepage dashboard by adding and arranging widgets](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/10.gif)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

There are 13 widgets to choose from — most are standard and work out of the box, while a few are flow-specific and need to be configured before they'll show data.

### Standard widgets

| Widget | Description |
|---|---|
| Items in your queue | Shows all items currently assigned to you. |
| Items in your queue – overdue | Shows items in your queue that have passed their due date. |
| Items in your queue – due today | Shows items in your queue that are due today. |
| Tagged comments | Displays comments where you've been tagged. |
| My to-do | Shows items you've marked as to-do from processes or boards assigned to you. |
| Recently accessed | Quick access to the items you opened most recently. |
| Access requests | Lists access requests awaiting your action. |
| Flow creation requests | Displays requests for creating new flows. |

<Callout type="info">
  **Items created by you** is also on the widget picker, but unlike the widgets above it needs a quick one-time configuration (setting a status filter) before it can be saved to your dashboard.
</Callout>

### Flow-specific widgets

These widgets need to be configured with a specific flow (and, for reports, a specific report) before they show data:

| Widget | Description |
|---|---|
| Items in your queue – specific flow | Shows items assigned to you from a chosen flow. |
| Items created by me – specific flow | Lists items you created in a specific flow. |
| Chart reports | Visual representation of a report from one of your flows. |
| Tabular reports | Tabular view of a report from one of your flows. |

<Callout type="warn">
  You can add up to 3 report widgets (chart or tabular), and up to 15 widgets in total, on your homepage.
</Callout>

Use **Reset** to discard unsaved changes and go back to your last saved dashboard. If you remove every widget and save, your dashboard reverts to the five default widgets rather than staying empty.

## Using AI auto suggest

Instead of building your dashboard widget by widget, use AI auto suggest from the widget picker:

- **Get suggestions** — AI surfaces widgets likely to be useful based on your activity and usage patterns. You just review the suggestions and place the ones you want.
- **Ask for widgets** — type what you're looking for, for example _items created by me in employee confirmation_, and AI configures the matching flow-specific widget for you to drag into place.

![Using AI auto suggest to get and configure widget recommendations](/migration-assets/homepage-overview/9.gif)

<Callout type="idea">
  AI auto suggest only recommends widgets from the standard 13-widget catalog — it doesn't create new widget types. For flow-specific widgets, it can go one step further and hand you a fully configured widget based on your request.
</Callout>
