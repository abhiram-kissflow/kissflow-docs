## What is Gmail?

Gmail is one of the most widely used email services. It keeps track of all your emails with threaded conversations, tags, and a Google-powered search to help you find any message.

## About Kissflow's Gmail integration

Kissflow Integration's Gmail connector lets you connect Gmail instantly with other Kissflow apps and popular third-party applications to automate your work. To integrate with Gmail, you must have a valid Gmail account and be a paid customer of Kissflow. You can use the Gmail connector to receive data from other flows or apps to perform specific actions in Gmail.

### Triggers in Gmail connector

**Trigger event**

**Description**

When a new email is received

Triggers when a new email is received in your Gmail account.

### Actions in Gmail connector

**Action event**

**Description**

Send an email

This action drafts and sends a new message from your Gmail account.

## Gmail connector as a trigger

1.  Create a new integration.
    
2.  Select the **Gmail** connector and then choose the trigger **When a new email is received**. You can also use the search to find the trigger directly.
    
3.  Create a new connection or use an existing connection in your account. You can connect to any Gmail account you have access to. The connection is authenticated using your Google credentials through OAuth. After authenticating the connection, click **Next**.
    
4.  Select the Gmail label (for example, Primary, Promotions) you want to monitor. Click **Next**.
    
5.  Test whether your trigger is set up correctly by clicking the **Test trigger** button. The connector will pull sample email data and display it in JSON format. If there's an error with the setup, you'll receive a message indicating the issue. Retest the trigger after making the necessary changes.

## Gmail connector as an action step

1.  In your Kissflow account, click the **Create** button in the left navigation panel and select **Integration** from the list. Enter a unique name and description for your integration and click **[Create](/t/60h4akx/creating-an-integration)**. 
2.  Once inside your editor, you can set up a trigger event in any Kissflow or third-party apps.
3.  After setting up your trigger step above, click the Add button (![](/migration-assets/integrate-with-gmail/1.png) ) next to set up your action step. Search for **Gmail** and select **Send an email** as the action.![](/migration-assets/integrate-with-gmail/2.png)  
4.  After choosing your action event, authenticate the connector. Click **+Add an account** and sign in using your Google account. Kissflow uses Gmail's OAuth for authentication.
5.  After authenticating with your Gmail account, add relevant values against the Gmail form fields. You can hit Refresh fields to view all the updated fields in the selected Gmail form. You can also map Gmail fields from your trigger step or fields from your previous action steps. These values will be automatically added to item fields after the action is executed.
6.  These are the mandatory and optional fields that you've to fill in your Gmail configuration form:
    -   **To**. These are the recipient(s) of the email. Add emails individually, or as comma seperated values. This is a mandatory field.
    -   **Cc and Bcc**. These are the addresses that should be cc'd and bcc'd on the email. Emails can be entered individually, or as comma seperated values. These are optional fields.
    -   **From**. This is the email address or alias from your Gmail account. This defaults to your primary email address. This is a mandatory field.
    -   **Subject and body**. You can add your custom subject line and body for your email here. These are mandatory fields.![](/migration-assets/integrate-with-gmail/4.png)
7.  After a successful configuration, test whether your Gmail action step is set up correctly by clicking the **T****est** button. The connector will pull field data from your selected Gmail account and display it as a JSON output. Similar to trigger step, errors are notified to you right away. Fix them before retesting the action step.

After creating a custom workflow with your Gmail connector's action steps, switch ON the toggle button to activate the integration run.
