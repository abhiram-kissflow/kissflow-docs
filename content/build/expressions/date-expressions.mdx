Date and datetime expressions are very similar but cannot be used interchangeably.  

Timezones and format settings for date fields are set at the account or user level.

Shows the absolute value of the difference between two dates based on a unit.

It will ignore non-working days by default. To count these, add false().

calendarDays

Calculates the inclusive difference between dates, counting both the start and end dates.  
 

Units

D (default): Total days

Y: Total years

M: Total months

HR: Total hours

MIN: Total minutes

SEC: Total seconds

MD: Remaining days, ignoring months

YM: Remaining months, ignoring years

YD: Remaining days, ignoring years

Day filters:

WD (Working days - default): Skips weekends/holidays.

AD (All days): Counts every day.

EW (Exclude weekends): Skips Sat/Sun only.

EH (Exclude holidays): Skips account holidays only.

calendarDays(date1, date2, “D”)

calendarDays(date1, date2, “D”, “AD”)

## Methods

**Function**

**Description**

**Syntax**

**Output**

**Output type**

offset

Offsets the date and time.

Y=year

M=month

D=day

h=hour

m=minutes

s=second

datetime1.offset(-10,"m")

Shows the last day of the month for the given field.

datetime1.eom()

Shows the week count for the given field.
