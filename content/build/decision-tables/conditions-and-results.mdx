# Creating conditions and results

<PlanBadge plans={["enterprise"]} />

Conditions are the criteria your decision table evaluates; results are the values it returns when a row matches.

## Create a condition

1. Click the **Add** button (+) beside **Conditions**.
2. Enter a name and choose the field type.
3. Optionally, set validations to control what values can be entered in the column.

### Supported field types

Conditions and results support these field types: **Text**, **Number**, **Date**, **Date & time**, **Dropdown**, **Yes/No**, **Currency**, **User**, and **Email**.

### Choosing operators

Each condition evaluates its values with an operator. Choose the operator mode that fits your logic:

- **Single** — evaluate with one operator. Available operators include `=`, `≠`, `>`, `>=`, `<`, `<=`, and for text fields, **Contains** and **Does not contain**.
- **Range** — evaluate values within a range by choosing a lower-limit operator (`>` or `>=`) and an upper-limit operator (`<` or `<=`). Range mode is available for **Number**, **Currency**, **Date**, and **Date & time** conditions.

**Example:** In a loan approval table, to evaluate credit scores between 600 and 800, choose **Range** with `>=` 600 and `<=` 800. To require a minimum score of 700, choose **Single** with `>=` 700.

## Create a result

1. Click the **Add** button (+) beside **Results**.
2. Enter a name and choose the field type.
3. Optionally, set validations for the result values.

After creating the result, enter the result values for each condition row.

![Create a result panel with Settings and Validation tabs, showing fields for name, field type, field ID, and help text](/migration-assets/creating-conditions-and-results/3.png)
{/* TODO: verify screenshot matches current product UI */}

## Editing columns

Click a condition or result column header to edit it. From the column menu you can also **Add column before**, **Add column after**, or delete the column.

## Concurrent editing

Only one person can edit a decision table at a time. If someone else is editing, you'll see a message that another developer is currently editing the decision table, and your changes are blocked until they finish. Their edits are auto-saved as they work.
