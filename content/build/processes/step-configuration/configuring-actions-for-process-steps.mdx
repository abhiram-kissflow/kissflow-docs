You can determine what action the assignee and the initiator of an item can take at each step.Go to the **Permissions** tab in the process editor. Select any step and scroll down to the bottom of the form. Click **Configure** **actions** and hover over the actions.

![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/5.png)

## Actions for new items

When a member of a process initiates a new item, they have two actions at the bottom. By default, these say **Submit** and **Discard**. 

![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/1.png)To configure this, click the step called **Start** and scroll to the bottom. You can choose to **Hide** or **Show the Discard action** to the initiator by default. The **Submit** button cannot be hidden because it’s the action that moves the item to the next step and must be shown.If you want to change the button captions, hover over either **Discard** or **Submit** and click **Advanced**. You can change the name to another option or click **Custom** to enter a new name.![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/2.png)In the **Advanced settings**, you can also add a validation based on the data in any field. The [field validations](/t/m1h4az4/field-validations) you configured in the form will also be in force, but this is a good option in case those values are dynamic throughout the workflow or if you want to use a [system field](/t/83h4alt/system-fields).

## Assignee actions for items in progress

At each step you can configure what actions can be taken by the assignee. ![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/3.png)Choose any step on the left menu. Scroll down the form and click **Configure actions**.   

-   **Submit** is the action that advances the workflow to the next step.
-   **Reassign** allows the current assignee to assign the item to a different user in your account. You can also restrict reassignment to specific users or users part of specific groups. Toggle **Restrict reassignment** and select a user or group from the drop-down.  
     ![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/6.png)
    
    > **Note:**
    > 
    > In the runtime, you can reassign an item only to one individual user at a time.
    > 
    > ![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/4.png)
    > 
    > Reassigning items in bulk will not be available when reassignment is restricted. This is because the system would need to check assignee eligibility for each item individually, which could lead to inconsistencies.
    
-   **Send** **back** allows the assignee to send an in-progress item back (with notes) to a previous step in the workflow.  
     
-   **Reject** sends an in-progress item back (with notes) to the initiator and cancels the workflow. Rejected items can be restarted, but are assigned a new [request number](/t/83h4alt/system-fields).   
      
    The rejection notes will be shown in the item form when you open the item from the **Created by me** and **Participated items** tabs.

Hovering over the options allows you to show, hide, or customize these buttons and also add validations.

## Initiator actions for items in progress

Once initiators submit an item, they can track it in the workflow through **My items**. When they open the item, they will see the fields configured at the **Start** step. At the bottom will be a set of actions they can take. You can configure these options under **Configure actions** and then select **Configure initiator actions**.![](/migration-assets/configuring-actions-for-process-steps/7.png)

-   **Reassign** allows the initiator to assign the task to a different assignee. 
-   **Withdraw** allows the initiator to remove the item from the workflow. 
-   **Restart** rejected and withdrawn items. The **Restart** button appears only when a request is withdrawn or rejected. It is always available by default and cannot be disabled.

Refresh fieldsTo know how to set refresh fields in the form at certain steps in the workflow, click [here](https://helpdocs.kissflow.com/permission-and-actions/refreshing-lookup-and-aggregation-field-values).

## Restricting the option to send-back items

You can decide to limit the number of preceding steps in your workflow to which an item can be sent back from a particular step in your process. This option allows additional control over the workflow. To enable this, turn on the toggle button and select the steps from the dropdown menu; the current step’s send-back functionality will then be limited to these steps.

> **Note:**
> 
> When you disable this option, items can be sent to all preceding steps.
