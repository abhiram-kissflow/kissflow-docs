You can collaborate with different stakeholders in a process workflow regarding an item using comments. You can also reply to people in a thread format so that you can keep the conversation in one place and won’t miss out on other important information. Apart from text, you can add emojis, attach files or images, you can [annotate the image](/t/h7ytkjrca) that you upload or the images uploaded by others, and mention people who are participants (initiator, step actors, and admins) in the process workflow.

In addition to adding comments, you can also like them or assign comments to the mentioned people. To add a comment to a process item, click the **Comments** tab in the right navigation strip of the item form. 

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/1.png)

> **Note:**
> 
> Irrespective of which role you’re in, you cannot delete a comment regardless of who added it.

## Mentioning users in a comment

To mention and tag a user, type **@** and spell the user's name in an item comment. You can only mention the current step's assignees, and previous step approvers of the item.

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/2.png)

Mentioning users in a comment will automatically notify them. However, you won't receive notifications if you mention yourself.

### Mentioning non-participants

You can mention non-participants in a comment, provided the relevant settings are enabled. This allows you to collaborate with external stakeholders while keeping the workflow controlled and secure. When you mention a non-participant in a comment, they,

-   Can view the item’s data as shown in the workflow step where they were mentioned.
    
-   Can read, reply, and react only within the comment thread where they were mentioned.
    
-   Can read all other past and future comments in the item.
    
-   Do not gain access to other steps, fields, or actions outside this context.
    
-   Are not added to the workflow or future steps.
    

> **Note**:
> 
> To mention a non-participant in a comment’s thread, they must first be mentioned in the parent comment.

Learn more about [setting comment permissions](/t/83ymdnx#comment-permissions).

## Assigning and resolving comments

You can assign a comment and its thread to a user if you have mentioned them in the comment. To assign a comment and its thread, mention a user who has participated in the workflow by entering **@** and their name > click the **Assign to** checkbox. The assignee will be notified of the assignment. 

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/7.gif)

If you have mentioned more than one user, you can assign the comment to only one of them from the drop-down beside the checkbox.

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/3.png)

You can resolve the comment by hovering over the comment and clicking the **Resolve** button (![](/migration-assets/process-comments/4.png)).

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/6.gif)

## Finding assigned comments

All your comment assignments will be shown under the **Assigned comments** tab on the process home page. From here, you can filter the open and resolved comments, view and resolve them, or even bulk-resolve them conveniently.

![](/migration-assets/process-comments/5.png)

## Assignment rules

-   If you have mentioned more than one user, you can assign the comment to only one of them from the drop-down beside the checkbox. 
-   You have to mention a participant in order to assign a comment to them.
-   A comment cannot be reassigned to another user once assigned.
-   Only the comment assignee and the user who initially assigned the comment can resolve the assignment as needed.
