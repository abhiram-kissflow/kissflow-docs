> As of November 18, 2024, the **Developer** user role has been removed as a part of the changes detailed in this [pre-release note](/t/60yzr6f/upcoming-changes-to-the-app-creation-flow). From now on, Apps will have only the **Admin** user role. In addition, **Developer settings** will no longer be available under **App settings**.

By default, an app created using Kissflow Low-code is private. It can only be accessed by the app owner, who is an admin of the app. The admin can share the entire app with individual users or groups, and assign admin or member permission to them. The admin can invite the others to view, edit, deploy, archive, and/or archive and delete the app.

The users and groups with whom the app is being shared may have different sets of permissions based on the scope of the work they have to accomplish with the app.

**The app developers with whom the app is shared:**

**Admins****:** Developers who can create, edit, deploy, archive, and delete an app.

**Members :** Individuals or groups who help the admin modify the app based on specific requirements. They will have create, edit, and deploy access.

![](/migration-assets/app-developers/1.png)

#### Sharing app with new developers

Developers are users in the Kissflow Low-code account who've additional permission to perform specific tasks within the app that you've created. A user with the admin or member permission is considered a co-owner of the app and has the same level of access to other sections as the app's administrator.

To share the app:

1.  Go to your app > click the **More Actions** icon in the top right corner > click **Share.**
2.  In the **Share** page, click **Add developers** > enter the name of the user or group that you want to add as an app developer.![](/migration-assets/app-developers/2.png)   
Assign **Admin** or **Member** permissions under **Role**. Click **Add**.

#### Managing admins and members

You can change the permission of an **Admin** or **Member** by accessing the developer card > clicking the **More options** button to make the required change. You can remove an admin or member from an app by clicking **Remove** button**.**
