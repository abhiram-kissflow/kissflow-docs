## Overview

Board views ensure alignment within your team by allowing you to focus on specific sets of data and share them effectively with your team members. 

Let’s say you have a Product release board where one team is focused on feature development while another handles feature testing. With board views, each team can create a filtered view that displays only the relevant items. 

As an app admin or a developer, you can create and save board views in [kanban](/t/60h9qlm/using-board-layouts#kanban), [list](/t/60h9qlm/using-board-layouts#list), [matrix](/t/60h9qlm/using-board-layouts#matrix), and [timeline](/t/60h9qlm/using-board-layouts#timeline) layouts and share them with the desired app roles. This makes it easier to work collaboratively with better context.

## Creating views

To create a new view,

-   Click **Views** > **\+ New view.**
    
-   Provide a view name and select the desired layout for your view.
    
    ![](/migration-assets/creating-and-using-board-views-in-apps/3.gif)
    
-   Sort based on fields.
-   Select the fields you want to display on the items under the **LAYOUT FIELDS** section. Certain system fields cannot be removed as they are required to remain visible.  
-   Add simple and advanced filter conditions under the **FILTER** section to track specific items on your unique views. 

> **Note:**
> 
> You cannot access the items while configuring the view.

Click the **Save** button at the top after you’re done with the configuration. 

## Sharing views

You can share a view with different roles in your app and assign them the following unique access permissions:

-   **Read-only** - Users will be able to view the items, but cannot edit them.
-   **Edit** - Users will be able to create, view, and update items.

To share a view,

1.  Click the **Share** button (![](/migration-assets/creating-and-using-board-views-in-apps/1.png) ) at the top right corner.
    
2.  Click **+** **Assign roles** and enter the app roles to which you want to give access and click **Confirm**.
    

![](/migration-assets/creating-and-using-board-views-in-apps/2.gif)

> **Note:** Please note that importing data to a board using a CSV file is not supported within Kissflow Apps.
